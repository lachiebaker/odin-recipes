Lentil Bolognese

Description

This delicious lentil bolognese is easy to make, healthy and most of all - cheap!
With only **10 ingredients**, you can make it with the things you have had sitting in your cupboard for months!.
Don't forget to use cheese!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ tablespoons of olive oil.
  • 1 large onion
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or shovel on more oregano)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt, plus more to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 tube of tomato paste
  • ½ cup dry red wine (optional)
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup red lentils, soaked
  • ¼ walnuts
  • 1 can of crushed tomatoes or whole peeled tomatoes
  • Lots of pasta
  • 1 tablespoon of high-quality balsamic vinegar
  • Flat-leaf Italian parley or fresh basil
  • Lots of parmesan to taste

Steps

  1. Soak the 1 cup of lentils in water for 30 minutes, or up to 60 minutes. Meanwhile prep all the other ingredients.
  2. Heat a 12-inch deep saute pan on medium-high heat. Add the olive oil, and once it's simmering, add the onions and season with a pinch of salt.
  3. Stir occasionally and cook the onions until a light brown fond starts to form on the surface of the pan, about 5 minutes. Add a few spoons of water to deglaze the pan, and stir.
  4. Add the garlic, thyme, oregano, 1 ½ teaspoons salt, and pepper to taste. Stir frequently and cook for 60-90 seconds
  5. Optional: If using red wine, pour the wine into the pan and deglaze, scraping up and browned bits. Cook for 1-2 minutes, until the smell of alcohol has burned off and the mixture is jammy.
  6. Pour in the broth to deglaze the pan, stirring any browned bits on the bottom of the pot and stirring the broth into the tomato paste to combine.
  7. Add the lentils and walnuts, and stir to incorporate. Heat until the mixture comes to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low to maintain a rapid simmer ofr 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Add the crushed tomatoes and simmer for another 15-20 minutes, or until the lentils are tender but still al dente, stirring occasionally to prevent burning and sticking
  9. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of water to the boil and salt. Add the pasta and cook until al dente.
  10. Add the hot cooked pasta to the bolognese and toss until evenly coated in the sauce, adding a bit of pasta water as needed to ensure the sauce coats the noodles.
